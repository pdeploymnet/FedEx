Our Team Activities

Modules

Module 1: What is Ethics; Conflicts faced

Ethics explores the logical reasons behind our moral judgments and investigates what actions are morally right or wrong, fair, or unfair. (Canada, 2015) In this module, participants will explore strategic concepts and their significance at FedEx. They will learn how ethics affects decision-making and will work across the organization. Participants will be encouraged to share their thoughts on negative practices that can occur in daily work, thus encouraging support and open discussion. These standards are designed to raise awareness of conflicts one may face and to emphasize the importance of ethical behavior in protecting FedEx's reputation as a reputable busines

What does ethics explore?

a) The financial performance and profitability of a company
b) The logical reasons behind our moral judgments and what actions are morally right or wrong, fair, or unfair
c) The physical office layout and design of a company
d) The marketing and advertising strategies of a company
Answer : a)


Module 2: Company’s Guidance Theory of Ethics

Ethical theories are methods for determining what is right and what is wrong. They instruct us on how we should act and make decisions. There are all sorts of ethical theories: those that focus on rules and those that emphasize the repercussions of our acts. (Larry Chonko, 2012) Module 2 will introduce FedEx's ethical principles, which can lead participants to important values such as honesty, trust, and customer focus. Participants will learn how to follow the company's mission and vision to promote and grow a culture of responsibility and ethics. This model will demonstrate the positive impact of following an ethical perspective on the company's reputation and stakeholder relations.

B. Overview of guiding ethics policy

This activity can provide participants with an overview of how the FedEx Guiding Ethics principles can be applied across various industries. They can look for real examples of how the company has acted ethically in the past. The model will encourage participants to consider how these ethical principles can be applied to their roles and decision-making processes. How they can train other employees and check their ethical behavior towards our customers.

What is the objective of the Module 2 activity?

a) To promote competition among employees
b) To provide an overview of how FedEx's Guiding Ethics principles can be applied across various industries
c) To encourage participants to ignore ethical principles in decision-making
d) To discourage cooperation and teamwork within the company
Answer : b


Module 3: Ethics - Our Team and Our Company

Work ethics refers to the way people work and the skills they need to do well. Successful individuals usually have these qualities. It includes things like being good at managing time, being reliable, disciplined, always on time, having a positive attitude, working hard, and being a team player. (Cooks-Campbell, 2022) Module 3 will address the importance of ethics within the FedEx team and throughout the organization. Participants can learn about the positive impact of ethical behavior on work teams, employee participation, and customer satisfaction. The model will emphasize that the principle of fairness can create a good working environment where conflict can be resolved appropriately. Moreover, this will impact our company’s values in a better way as rules and regulations will be for everyone inside the company.

B. Addressing Ethical Challenges According to Departments

This section will describe the ethical challenges faced by various departments within FedEx. Participants can learn about how ethical considerations can vary between departments and how teams work together to resolve conflicts. This model will emphasize the importance of effective communication and collaboration in reducing ethical challenges.

What does the principle of fairness create within the organization, according to the model?

a) A competitive work environment
b) A working environment where conflicts cannot be resolved
c) A good working environment where conflicts can be resolved appropriately
d) A culture of strict rules and discipline
Answer: c)


Module 4: Leadership - How the Company Works; How to Motivate Employees

Leadership is the skill of a person or a group to guide and influence the members of an organization, society, or team. (Barney, 2023) There are three styles of modules that are under as follows: 1. Authoritarian Leaders are demanding and don’t allow negotiation, useful in urgent situations. 2. Participative Leadership involves subordinates in decision-making, motivating them. 3. Bureaucratic Leadership relies on hierarchical order and strict rules. (FedEx, n.d.) Lesson 4 will focus on the role of leadership in boosting values at FedEx. Participants will learn about the company's ethical culture and how leaders behave. This model will highlight the importance of managers providing support and guidance to employees in solving problems and handling customer issues.

B) Management Style - Commitment to Social Responsibility

This section will explore how FedEx integrates social responsibility ethics into its management style. Participants can learn about the company's commitment to environmental sustainability, community engagement, and employee health. This model will show how this commitment is reflected in the company's management decisions.

What will the section "Management Style - Commitment to Social Responsibility" explore?

a) The company's commitment to increasing profits and revenue
b) How FedEx integrates social responsibility ethics into its management style
c) The history of bureaucratic leadership at FedEx
d) The marketing campaigns used to promote FedEx's services
Answer : b)


Module 5 Corporate Culture – We Defend/Encourage/Enable/Develop/Grow

Corporate culture is the combination of values, beliefs, ethics, and attitudes that defines an organization and shape how it operates. (Pratt, 2020) Module 5 will examine FedEx's corporate culture and how it aligns with it. Participants will learn about the key principles that the company advocates in its culture, pros, and cons. This model will demonstrate the positive impact of good leadership on employee satisfaction and customer relations. Corporate Governance in Our Company In this section, FedEx's corporate governance structure will be explained. Participants can learn about the roles and responsibilities of the board, leadership, and other management in promoting ethical standards and ensuring compliance. These standards can address the transparency and accountability of FedEx's corporate governance.

What is the objective of the activities in Module 5?

a) Encourage employees to ignore the company's culture
b) Promote a negative company culture
c) Allow participants to share their perspectives on the development of corporate ethics and ethical decision-making at all levels
d) Promote unhealthy competition among employees
Answer : C)


Module 6: Commitment to international ethics; what we do and what we want.

International ethics is a field in international relations theory that deals with the ethical responsibilities and obligations between countries in a globalized world. (Wikipedia, 2022) Module 6 will focus on FedEx's global commitment to ethical behavior. Participants can learn about the initiatives and policies implemented by companies worldwide to ensure ethical business practices. The module will address cultural awareness and the importance of complying with local laws and regulations.

Why is cultural awareness important in international ethics?

a) To ensure FedEx's employees dress appropriately in different regions
b) To avoid misunderstandings and conflicts due to cultural differences
c) To promote a homogeneous global culture within FedEx
d) To encourage employees to only interact with people from their own culture
Answer: b)
